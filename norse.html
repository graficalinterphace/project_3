Introduction to Norse Gods

This page will provide a non-comprehensive list of the most major Norse gods (as described in myths) and provide a breif summary covering them. There is no single comprehensive canon in Norse mythology, so this page is based on the most popular source of myths, the Prose Edda.

The Æsir

This is the the "primary" group of gods detailed in the myths. Most of the stories cover their lives. They reside in Asgard.

Odin

  • Leader of the gods. God of war, wisdom, and poetry.
  • Constantly seeks wisdom and knowledge of the future. Sacrificed one eye in the pursuit of further wisdom.

Thor

  • God of thunder and rain. Protects gods and humans alike, usually from giants.

Tyr

  • God of conflicts and contracts.
  • Gave up one hand in order to bind the monstrous wolf Fenrir.

Baldr

  • God of light and peace. Said to be the most beautiful of the gods.
  • Unintentionally killed by his blind brother Höðr. Will return after Ragnarök, the end of the world, to rule over an age of peace.

Bragi

  • Poet of the gods. May be based on a real historical poet, Bragi Boddason.

Heimdall

  • Watchman of the gods. The blowing of his horn will signal the beginning of Ragnarök.
  • One of the oldest gods, he is thought to have fathered humanity and its caste system.

Frigg

  • Queen of the gods. Aids in childbirth.
  • Can see into the future, but is incredibly secretive.

Iðunn

  • Goddess of youth and life. Guards apples that keep the gods from aging.

The Vanir

A rival family of gods. After a war with the Æsir, they have come to a truce, exchanging important hostages.

Njörð

  • The god of sea and plenty. Brings good harvests and prosperity.
  • Father of Freyr and Freja.

Freyr

  • God of fertility. Brings peace and prosperity.

Freja

  • Goddess of fertility, love, magic, and death.
  • She is the most complex of goddesses, directly comparable Odin in many aspects.

Giants

Known as "jötunn", these giants are not neccessarily large. As a tribe they are in conflict with the Æsir, and are not classified as gods. However, a few specific individuals have been accepted as gods:

Loki

  • Son of a giant, and sworn blood brother of Odin. Often used for comedy, but is explicitly wicked.
  • Selfishly causes problems for the gods, but fixes them when threatened with bodily harm.

Skaði

  • Frost giantess who joins gods after marrying Njörð.
  • Goddess of skiing and hunting.
